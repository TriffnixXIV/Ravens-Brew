To convert an already built character from the DnD 5e2024 rules to this homebrew ruleset, follow the steps listed here. You can find more information in the linked chapters.
### Martial Stuff
**_[[AC-less Defence]]_**: You will not need your AC anymore. You can keep it for now to remind yourself which features might give you Damage Reduction instead.

**_[[AC-less Defence|Hit DC]]_**: Check your size. Note down the Hit DC associated with that size. Your Hit DC is 10 if you are Medium and 12 if you are Small.

**_[[Species#Small Characters|Small Characters]]_**: If you are Small, you gain a +2 bonus to all attack rolls, a -1 malus to weapon damage rolls, and your Hit Dice are reduced to the next smaller die. To update your Hit Point Maximum accordingly, reduce it by a number equal to your level, then reduce it by 1.

**_[[Armour]]_**: Check the armour you own. Convert the AC it gives into Damage Reduction following the rules given in the chapter on [[Armour]]. You have a full set of each armour you own. Also note each armours modifier to your [[Dodging]] checks.

**_[[Weapons]]_**: Check the weapons you own. Remove the Proficiency Bonus from your attack rolls, as weapons now work through Training. You can get the Proficiency Bonus back later through Martial Training.

Note your [[Parrying]] Bonus and Parrying Power for each weapon. The Parrying Bonus is equal to the ability modifier used for attacks, the Parrying Power is equal to the maximum damage you deal with a regular attack.

**_[[Shields]]_**: If you have a shield, note a [[Blocking]] Modifier of +4 for it.

**_[[Martial Training]]_**: Check how many Weapon Mastery Properties you have access to. Convert each of them into a Martial Training feature. Similarly, check your Fighting Style feats and convert each into a Martial Training feature. There should be an equivalent to each Weapon Mastery Property and each Fighting Style feat, but you can pick any Martial Training feature you want. Some features give bonuses to the new [[Blocking|block]], [[Dodging|dodge]] and [[Parrying|parry]] reactions.

Lastly, check the [[Feats]] you have and whether they've been converted into Martial Training features. If they were converted, replace it with the Martial Adept Feat and pick two Martial Training features accordingly. There should be Martial Training features equivalent to the Feats benefits.

**_[[Classes|Class Feature Changes]]_**: Check whether your class features were changed and note the new versions if applicable.
### Caster Stuff
If you are a spellcaster, also follow these steps. Note your total spellcaster level.

**_[[Spell Points]] and [[Spell Limits]]_**: You now use Spell Points and Spell Limits instead of Spell Slots. You have a Spell Point Maximum equal to six times your spellcaster level, and Spell Limits of 3 at level 1, increasing by 3 every two levels afterwards. On the levels inbetween, you can interpolate the Spell Limits linearly.

**_[[Spellcasting Focus]]_**: Check each Spellcasting Focus you own. Each one is now associated with a number of spell schools, as described in their chapter. Note these schools.

**_[[Caster Training]]_**: Choose a number of [[Caster Training]] features equal to the Proficiency Bonus you'd have if it was derived from your caster level.
#### Spells
Go through your spells. Do the following for each:

**_[[Spell Check|Spell checks]]_**: Note the bonus you have on the Spell check you now roll when casting it. The bonus is equal to the relevant spellcasting ability modifier, and if you have a Spellcasting Focus that is associated with the school of the spell, you add your Proficiency Bonus as well.

**_DC modifier_**: If the spell requires a saving throw, note a DC modifier of minus 2 if it targets one creature, and minus 4 if it targets multiple. This is the number you modify the Spell check by to find out the DC that the targets have to roll their saving throws against.

**_Spell Point Cost_**: Note how many Spell Points it costs. The Spell Point cost is equal to three times the spells level.

**_[[Spells|Spell Changes]]_**: Check whether the spell was changed. Most changes were made to spells that interact with [[Spells#Reworked Spells - Defence|defence]] or work [[Spells#Reworked Spells - Spell checks|against other spells]], otherwise, many offensive spells had their [[Spells#Spells with adjusted Upcasting|upcasting changed]].
