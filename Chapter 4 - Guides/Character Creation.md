To create a new character using this homebrew ruleset, follow the steps listed here. You can find more information in the linked chapters.
### Class
Choose your class as usual, but adapt the following details:

**_Weapon Proficiency Conversion_**: Instead of getting Proficiency with certain weapons based on the class you choose, you receive Training with these weapons. To add the Proficiency Bonus to certain attack rolls, you can take the [[Martial Training]] feature _Accuracy_ if you get the chance.

**_More Skills_**: There are now two additional skills, [[Blocking]] and [[Parrying]]. Proficiency with those skills is accessible to every character that can choose freely between all skills, and to those that receive [[Martial Training]]. [[Dodging]] is also new, though it works through Acrobatics. Note your modifier for these three skills.

**_Weapon Mastery Property Conversion_**: If you get access to a number of Weapon Mastery Properties, you receive the same amount of [[Martial Training]] features instead. If you get access to another Weapon Mastery Property later, you will instead get another Martial Training feature.

**_Fighting Style Conversion_**: If you get a Fighting Style feat, you receive a [[Martial Training]] feature instead.

**_Class Feature Changes_**: Some [[Classes|classes]] received changes to their features to bring those in line with this homebrew. Check the section on your class to make sure you adapt those features accordingly.

**_Spellcasting Changes_**: [[Spellcasting]] has been fundamentally changed, so you no longer track spell slots, but how many [[Spell Points]] you have at your disposal and your [[Spell Limits]]. At level 1, you have 6 Spell Points and Spell Limits of 3.

**_Spell Changes_**: When choosing spells, note their spell school and their Spell Point cost. The Spell Point cost is equal to three times a spells level. The spell school is important to determine to which [[Spell Check|Spell checks]] you add your Proficiency Bonus, depending on the [[Spellcasting Focus]] you use. Consider which spell schools you want to emphasize and choose your Spellcasting Focus accordingly.

**_Caster Training_**: If you play a spellcaster, you get two [[Caster Training]] features in the beginning. Afterwards, note your spellcaster level. At spellcaster level 5, 9, 13 and 17, you receive another Caster Training feature each.

**_Level 5 Surge Die Feature_**: If you are at least level 5 in a class, you receive a new feature that relates to Surge Dice in some capacity. You can find these features in the [[Classes|class]] chapter as well.
### Origin
Choose your Species, Origin and languages as usual, but adapt the following detail:

**_Tool Proficiency Conversion_**: Instead of getting Proficiency with a set of tools, you receive Training with it instead. To add the Proficiency Bonus to an ability check with a set of tools, the skill you roll the check with matters now.
### Equipment
Choose your Equipment as usual, but adapt the following details:

**_Parrying_**: Note your [[Parrying]] Bonus and Parrying Power for each weapon. The Parrying Bonus is equal to the ability modifier used for attacks, the Parrying Power is equal to the maximum damage you deal with a regular attack.

**_New Equipment_**: Take note of the new options for [[Shields]] and the possibility of buying [[Armour#Armour Pieces|Armour Pieces]] instead of full sets of armour.

**_Armour Changes_**: [[Armour]] gives Damage Reduction instead of an AC increase now. The main armour you wear also determines a modifier to your [[Dodging]] checks. Note this modifier.

**_Blocking_**: Note your [[Blocking]] Bonus for the shield you use.

**_Spellcasting Focus_**: Your [[Spellcasting Focus]] determines to which Spell checks you add your Proficiency Bonus, as noted above and described there.
### Rest
Determine your ability scores, choose your alignment, and fill in the details as usual. When leveling up the character, keep the changes noted above in mind, otherwise, the process works the same.
